## 2) Stable vs speculative (practical comparison)

### Stable cashflow models (templates/services/directories)
**Pros**
- Repeatable systems, controllable inputs
- Lower blow-up risk
- Compounding through catalog expansion, SEO, repeat clients

**Cons**
- Slower start
- Requires operations discipline (customer support, updates, optimization)

**Best use:** Primary income engine.

### Speculative (prediction markets/trading)
**Pros**
- Fast feedback, possible upside bursts
- Small edge can scale if truly systematic

**Cons**
- High variance, emotional risk, regime shifts
- Most operators underperform after fees/slippage/taxes
- Can distract from building durable assets

**Best use:** Small sandbox only, never core livelihood.

## 3) Risk caps & position sizing rules

Use hard caps so one mistake doesn’t kill momentum:

### Portfolio-level caps
- **Max monthly loss (total): 8% of deployable business capital**
  - Hit cap ⇒ pause speculation + paid tests until next month review
- **Emergency reserve:** hold **6 months personal runway** outside this portfolio
- **Tax reserve:** sweep **25–35% of net profit** to separate tax account

### Stable-model caps
- New product/channel test: **1–3%** of monthly capital each
- Kill/iterate threshold: if no traction by predefined KPI window (e.g., 30–45 days), stop or pivot

### Speculative caps
- **Total speculative sleeve:** 0–10% (as above)
- **Per trade/position risk:** **0.25–0.75%** of total capital
- **Daily loss limit:** **1.5%** of total capital
- **Weekly loss limit:** **3%** of total capital
- **Max open correlated risk:** **2%** total
- No averaging down losers; no leverage for conservative profile

## 4) Stop-loss & drawdown protocol (simple rules)

1. **Hard stop per position** (predefined invalidation, no “mental stops”).
2. **3-loss rule:** after 3 consecutive losses, stop for 24h and review logs.
3. **Drawdown throttle:**
   - Down 5% from equity high → cut position size by 50%
   - Down 8% → stop speculative activity for rest of month
4. **Re-entry only after review checklist passed** (strategy edge, execution error, market regime).

For stable models, “stop-loss” means **time/KPI stop**:
- Example: If listing/page doesn’t hit minimum CTR/conversion target by day 45, improve once; if still below by day 60, retire.

## 5) US legal/compliance: clear YES/NO list

## ✅ Generally YES (with proper setup/disclosures)
- Selling original templates, digital products, and services
- Running directory/affiliate sites with clear disclosures (FTC)
- Using licensed assets per terms
- Registering business properly, collecting/remitting taxes as required
- Prediction market participation **only where legally permitted** and per platform rules/KYC

## ❌ Avoid entirely
- Insider trading / trading on material nonpublic info
- Market manipulation (wash trading, spoofing, fake volume/signals)
- Unregistered securities solicitation or “guaranteed returns” pitches
- Copying/ripping copyrighted templates, PLR abuse beyond license
- Fake reviews/testimonials, undisclosed paid endorsements (FTC risk)
- Operating unlicensed money transmission / custody schemes
- Tax evasion, hidden offshore structures for concealment
- VPN/geofence bypass to access prohibited gambling/trading venues
- Using bots that violate platform terms in ways that trigger fraud/abuse liability
